Amazon Web Services (AWS)

Launched in 2006, Amazon Web Services, Inc. began providing key infrastructure services to businesses in the form of web services -- now widely known as cloud computing.

The ultimate benefit of cloud computing, and AWS, is the ability to leverage a new business model and turn capital infrastructure expenses into variable costs. Businesses no longer need to plan and procure servers and other IT resources weeks or months in advance. Using AWS, businesses can take advantage of Amazon's expertise and economies of scale to access resources when their business needs them, delivering results faster and at a lower cost. 

Today, Amazon Web Services provides a highly reliable, scalable, low-cost infrastructure platform in the cloud that powers hundreds of thousands of enterprise, government and startup customers businesses in 190 countries around the world. Amazon Web Services offers over 40 different services, including Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud (Amazon EC2), Amazon Simple Storage Service (Amazon S3) and Amazon Relational Database Service (Amazon RDS). AWS services are available to customers from data center locations in the U.S., Brazil, Europe, Japan, Singapore and Australia.

Learn more at http://aws.amazon.com or Contact AWS TW Account Manager Locarno Pan locarnop@amazon.com or James Tien jtien@amazon.com.

Archilife Research Foundation

Since its establishment on December 30, 1978, the Archilife Research Foundation has offered scholarships to support students in master courses to complete their theses, and implemented different ways to integrate knowledge. After long years of its pioneering stage, Archilife commenced various long-term researches in the areas of architecture, environmental sustainability and development starting from 1987. During the process of researching human inhabitations and sustainable development, Archilife discovered that advancements of information technologies and cyberspace over the past decade had profound impacts on human lifestyles. The sharing and integration of related knowledge, and the education in these fields should be one of Archilife's essential objectives. Therefore, besides scholarships regarding architecture, environmental sustainability, ecology and health care, Archilife offered scholarships for the theses related to information society and culture from 2000 to 2008 as well. On the other hand, information technologies and the Internet brought new possibilities to how people can express their creativities and interact with each other. So, subsequent to recent adjustments of the scholarships and the funding projects, Archilife started with holding a series of sharing and exchanging meet-ups among the domestic independent game developers' community, conveying and communicating the knowledge and experiences learned from certain international game development conferences, in the hope that it would help this community to evolve. About other aspects of knowledge integration, Archilife developed different methods including: a knowledge bank system, index books, and knowledge keepers in various fields. These resources have served as powerful tools in filling in research gaps and refining research findings. Archilife currently holds periodic communions and bookish assemblies to strengthen the knowledge of its members so as to face a heterogeneous and changing future.

Delivering Open Innovations for Tomorrow

DOIT,　which stands for Delivering Open Innovations for Tomorrow, is a project supported by Taiwan's Department of Industrial Technology, Ministry of Economic Affairs (DOIT, MOEA) and carried out by the Market Intelligence & Consulting Institute, Institute for Information Industry (MIC, III). Believing in the power of open innovation, DOIT gathers innovators and helps them find required resources to realize their ideas. In the same way, DOIT also aims to create an open atmosphere in the industry and help enterprises gain innovation energy from the outside. During the course of establishing such a platform, we have met many different professionals. Despite their disparate backgrounds and values, they all agree with the notion that the time when one can solely rely on his or her own to make a difference has gone. Nowadays, the pace of innovation led by individuals can barely catch up with the ever-changing world. Collaborated innovation is therefore the new black. It can be a small-scale cooperation among people in the same field, a large-scale cross-disciplinary co-creation, or even teamwork among various international groups through the Internet. Openness and sharing is changing the face of the world. Creativity organizations everywhere have been keen to create a brave new world in their own way. Against this backdrop, DOIT, as an innovation intermediaries, is founded in Taiwan in the hope of linking Taiwan's innovators and encouraging the industry to embrace the idea of open innovation.  More About DOIT, please visit official site: https://taiwan-doit.com/

科技新報 （TechNews）

科技新報 （TechNews）是一群對資訊科技、能源、半導體、行動運算、網際網路、醫療、生物科技具有高度熱忱與興趣的產業與新媒體人士所共同組成的時代新媒體，以產出有觀點與特色的原創文章為主要任務，提供多語言版本與深度化內容，並且走向國際市場，每日持續產生對人類未來有貢獻的相關文章與報導。

在科技新報，我們不只是報導有價值、有意義的資訊，同時也會有具備全局觀點、個人觀點及評論刊載，為市場和業內人士關心的趨勢、內幕與新聞，這些內容與資訊絕大部分都來自於長期觀察與業界人脈累積而來。

數位時代

《數位時代媒體群》創立於1999年7月，跨越20世紀，走過科技發展的關鍵年代，見證網路新經濟風起雲湧的演進歷程，我們持續探索、記錄、解讀全球正在發生，和即將發生的數位革命與變動。

《數位時代媒體群》為讀者尋找影響下一步的「新觀念」，描繪正在形成的「新環境」，記錄開創未來的「新典範」，深掘新商業模式的「新機會」及「新價值」，為讀者早一步解讀趨勢脈動，領先掌握下一步行動的競爭優勢。

凝聚全台最具影響力的科技菁英社群，集結台灣最具創造力的開發者及創業菁英，成為全台灣最重要的科技財經社群及知識傳播平台。
